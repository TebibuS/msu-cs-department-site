Apply to the Upper-Division in Computer Science

Program Overview

Computer Science welcomes new students to our innovative project-based program! Lower-division courses follow the common computing core shared among several technology-focused majors, complemented by courses that will earn you a minor in mathematics.

The upper-division program is where theory meets practice. Students work on a series of four significant, semester-long projects drawn from real-world industry challenges and cutting-edge research. You will typically collaborate in teams to complete projects and have the option of pursuing a senior thesis before graduation.

The Application Process

Admission into the upper-division requires a formal application. First-year students should start by following the major's recommended four-year plan.

Current Minnesota State Mankato Students: Apply with this form.

Transfer Students: Apply with this application.

Application Materials

You will be asked to include the following in your application:

  • Unofficial transcript(s).
  • An essay (300-500 words) describing why you want to participate in this project-based Computer Science program.
  • An essay (300-500 words) describing how you will contribute to the learning communities that develop within our program.
  • Sample code (25-100 lines) from a personal project or your Algorithms class, with a brief description (under 100 words) of what the code does.
  • Either one letter of recommendation addressing your ability to succeed in this program, or the name of a current College of Science, Engineering, & Technology faculty member you have spoken with about your interest.
    (It is strongly recommended that your recommender is a professor in math, science, or computing, or an industry mentor).

Recommenders can send letters directly to Dr. Rushit Dave at rushit.dave@mnsu.edu.
